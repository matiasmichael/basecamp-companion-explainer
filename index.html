Companion, Not Agent

Most AI tools either chat with you or do tasks for you. A Basecamp companion does both — and the combination is what makes it actually useful.

🧠

It Knows You

Your companion maintains files about you — who you are, how you communicate, what you're working on, what decisions you've made. These aren't hidden system prompts. They're real documents it reads and updates over time, like notes a good executive assistant would keep.

Under the Hood

There's a SOUL file that shapes personality, a USER file with your preferences and context, and MEMORY files that grow over time. The companion manages these itself — you just talk to it normally.

⚡

It Gets Things Done

Web search, writing code, managing files, drafting emails, scheduling meetings, browsing websites, running automations. It doesn't just tell you what to do — it does the work.

Why the Combination Matters

Because it knows your context, the work it does is actually good. When it drafts an email, it sounds like you. When it researches something, it knows what's relevant to your situation. Context makes the output useful instead of generic.

What a Typical Day Looks Like

This isn't something you pull up when you're stuck. It's just there, running, throughout the day.

7:30 AM

Morning Briefing

Before you even open your laptop, your companion has already gone through overnight emails, Slack, and your calendar. You get a summary of what actually matters — not everything, just the stuff you'd want to know about.

9:00 AM

Quick Research

You mention a company you might want to partner with. The companion pulls up their website, recent news, funding history, and puts together a one-page brief. You didn't have to write a detailed prompt — it already knows what kind of info you care about.

11:30 AM

Drafting Emails

You need to follow up with three investors. You give the companion the gist of each one, it writes the drafts. They sound like you because it's been reading your emails for weeks and knows your tone.

2:00 PM

Keeping Track of Things

You forgot about a deliverable due tomorrow. The companion didn't. It also noticed a task that's been blocked for three days and flags it — you decide what to do about it.

4:30 PM

Working on a Deck

You're rethinking the positioning for a pitch. You talk it through with the companion, go back and forth on messaging, and it starts building out a slide structure while you jump on another call.

7:00 PM

End of Day

The companion saves everything from today to its memory files — what you decided, what got done, what's still open. Tomorrow it picks up exactly where you left off. No "remind me where we were."

How It's Different

If you've used ChatGPT or Claude, you'll notice some things work differently here.

1

It Actually Remembers

Not "memory" where it vaguely recalls something from three weeks ago. Real files on disk that it reads every session. Tell it something once, it knows it forever.

→
2

It Has an Identity

There are actual files that define who your companion is, what it knows about you, and how it should behave. You can shape these by just... talking to it about what you want.

→
3

It Does Real Work

It has a terminal, a browser, access to your tools. When you say "send that email" or "look this up," it's actually executing — not generating a draft for you to copy-paste somewhere.

→
4

Someone's Looking After It

The Basecamp team keeps your companion updated, fixes issues, and adds new capabilities over time. You don't have to maintain anything yourself.
