Companion, Not Agent

Most AI products do one of two things: chat (being) or execute tasks (doing). A Basecamp companion does both.

🧠

The "Being" Side

Your companion remembers everything. Not in a database — in actual files it reads at the start of every conversation. Your preferences, communication style, ongoing projects, decisions you've made, context from last week or last month.

How It Works

A SOUL file defines the companion's personality. A USER file captures who you are and how you work. MEMORY files accumulate over time. These aren't prompts — they're persistent documents the companion maintains itself.

⚡

The "Doing" Side

Your companion has access to real tools: web search, code execution, file management, email, calendar, messaging, browser automation. It doesn't suggest actions — it performs them.

Why Both Matter

Because it knows your context, the actions it takes are relevant. It doesn't just send an email — it sends the right email, in your voice, to the right person, because it knows the project history.

What a Day Looks Like

A companion isn't something you open when you need help. It's running alongside you, all day.

7:30 AM

Morning Briefing

Your companion reviews overnight emails, Slack messages, and calendar. It summarizes what matters and flags anything that needs your attention before your first meeting.

9:00 AM

Research Task

You ask it to research a potential partner company. It searches the web, reads their site, checks recent news, and puts together a brief — cited, structured, ready for your review.

11:30 AM

Email Drafting

Three emails to investors need to go out. You give it the key points; it writes the drafts in your voice — because it's read dozens of your previous emails and knows how you communicate.

2:00 PM

Project Tracking

Your companion updates the project board, checks in on blocked tasks, and reminds you about a deliverable due tomorrow you'd forgotten about.

4:30 PM

Creative Collaboration

You're reworking a pitch deck. You brainstorm with the companion, iterate on positioning, and it builds a first draft of the new slides structure while you're on another call.

7:00 PM

End of Day

It logs everything to memory — decisions made, tasks completed, open threads. Tomorrow morning, it picks up exactly where you left off.
