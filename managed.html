🛡️ Handled by Basecamp

We Maintain It, You Just Use It

A companion isn't something you install and figure out on your own. We run the infrastructure, handle the security side, and keep improving how it works. You just get the benefit of having it around. Here's what that actually looks like.

What "Managed" Actually Means

A few ways this is different from just hooking up an API yourself.

🖥️

It Has Its Own Space

Your companion runs on dedicated infrastructure. It has its own workspace, files, and state. You aren't sharing an endpoint with thousands of other users — it's yours.

👥

We Watch and Fix

We keep an eye on how the companions are performing. When something breaks or could be done better, we push an update. You don't have to file tickets or wait for a big release.

🤝

It Gets Better Automatically

Because we're constantly refining skills and adding new capabilities to the platform, your companion naturally gets smarter over time without you having to do anything.

Behind the Scenes

Here's what our team does so you don't have to.

🔒

Keeping Things Secure

We add a layer of security over the base AI. That means checking for sensitive info leaks, protecting your data, and enforcing safe web browsing (like using DuckDuckGo). We keep your stuff yours.

📋

Building New Skills

When a companion stumbles on a task, we build a "Skill" (a set of instructions) to make sure it knows how to handle it next time. When we fix a workflow for one person, everyone's companion gets the update.

🎯

Polishing How It Talks

We spend time reviewing how the system responds. Is it too wordy? Does it miss the point? We tweak the underlying behavior so it communicates more clearly over time.

⚙️

Handling the Tech

When new models come out or servers need scaling, we handle the migration. You just keep talking to your companion while we swap out the engine underneath.

👁️

Human Oversight

This isn't a set-and-forget piece of software. We have real people reviewing the system and making calls about what needs to be improved.

🔄

System Upgrades

Beyond what the companion learns from you directly, we're constantly pushing better defaults and smarter ways to handle common tasks to the whole platform.

Why We Call It a Companion

We use the word "companion" instead of "agent" for a reason.

An agent is transactional. You give it a task, it does the task. A companion does the task too, but it also has context on who you are. It learns how you like things done, remembers your past conversations, and grows alongside you.

To pull this off, the companion has actual memory files. It doesn't rely on a database trick to guess what you were talking about last week. It keeps readable notes about you, your projects, and decisions you've made, and it checks those notes before it answers you.

It also has a SOUL file. This dictates its personality. If you want it to be more direct, or to stop apologizing, or to always format lists a certain way — you just tell it. It updates its own SOUL file. Over time, it stops feeling like generic software and starts feeling like *yours*.

It's the difference between using a tool and working with a partner.

What to Expect

A few quick points to clarify what this is (and isn't).

  • ✕
    It isn't just ChatGPT with a new UI.

    ChatGPT starts fresh every time you talk to it. A Basecamp companion has its own workspace, its own persistent files, and tools it can actually run. The plumbing underneath is completely different.

  • ✕
    It's not a chatbot.

    Chatbots are for answering questions. A companion is for doing work. It browses the web, writes code, manages files, and sends messages for you.

  • ✕
    You don't have to manage it.

    There's no complex setup or software to install. We handle the infrastructure and maintenance — you just use it.

  • ✕
    It won't stay the same.

    Because we're actively managing the platform, the companion you have today will be noticeably smarter and more capable in a few months, just from the updates we push.
